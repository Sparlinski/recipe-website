CHICKEN MARSALA

Marsala wine defines both the name of this dish and its flavor profile. However, it is also higher in sugar than other wines and thus this variation of the recipe takes the best elements of the traditional dish and augments it to give a lower sugar profile. By swapping out the marsala wine for red wine vingar you can reduce the sugar content without sacrificing any of the flavors.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 pounds chicken breast
  • 1 onion
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 trays of button mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 2 tablspoons of parsley
  • 4 tablespoons of flour
  • 4 tablespoons of butter
  • 1/2 cup of red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon concentrated chicken broth
  • 1 cup of cream

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 30mins

  1. Start by cutting the chicken breasts half-length ways.
  2. Add the chicken to a ziplock bag, and add a teaspoon of salt and pepper and the flour to the bag. Close the bag and turn the chicken repeatedly until the chicken breasts are evenly coated with the seasoning. Set aside the remaining flour for later.
  3. Melt 2 tablespoons of butter in a medium-high heat pan then add the chicken. Fry one side of the chicken for 5 minutes, then flip and fry for another 3 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside on a plate.
  4. While your chicken is on the pan, start on the onions, garlic and mushrooms. Cut the onions, dice the garlic, and wash and slice the mushrooms.
  5. When the chicken is finished, reduce the heat to medium and add the remaining butter to the pan. Let it melt scrape the remnants of the chicken and mix it with the butter.
  6. Add the mushrooms and saute them for 2-3 mins, add the onions and garlic, saute for another 2 minutes, then add the wine, cream, chicken broth, and parsley to the pan. Stir the sauce and bring to a gentle boil.
  7. If your pan is deep enough and can be covered, add the chicken to it, if not add the chicken and the sauce to a suitable pot.
  8. With the chicken and sauce combined, bring the sauce back to a gentle boil. Check the consistency of the sauce. If it is too thin, mix a tablespoon or 2 of the remaining seasoned flour set aside from earlier with a little water to form a paste. Add it to the sauce. Cover the pot or pan, reduce the heat to medium-low, and let it simmer for 5 minutes. Check on its consistency and decide if it needs to be tickened a little more. If you are happy with its consistency, cover the pot or pan and let it simmer for 10 more minutes. Serve with a choice of vegetables, like roasted green beans or roasted asparagus, and pair with pasta or mashed potatoes and enjoy.
