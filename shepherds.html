SWEET POTATO SHEPARDS PIE

Shepherd's pie is a simple and heartwarming meal. This version has reduced the richer ingredients you find throughout the various approaches to the dish. It is a stripped-down clean version that still brings that heartwarming taste with a flavorsome mince and vegetable filling and a creamy, sweet mash topping with a delightful roasted crisp finish.

INGREDIENTSserves 6

  • 2 pounds of mince
  • 2.5 pounds of sweet potato
  • 1 onion
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 4 carrots
  • 2 cups of peas
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon of pepper
  • 2 tablspoons of oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of thyme
  • 1 tablespoon of concentrated beef broth
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 3 tablespoons of Irish butter

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 1 hour

  1. Set the oven to 380deg farenheit.
  2. Put a pot of water large enough to hold the potatoes on high heat. Season with salt.
  3. Peel and wash the sweet potatoes. Dice them and add to the water when it is boiling. Return to a boil, cover, and reduce heat to medium-low. Let simmer for 25 mins while you prepare the mince filling.
  4. Finely dice your onion, garlic, and carrots.
  5. Add a large pot to the heat and add 2 tablespoons of butter. When the butter has melted, add the onion and soften in the butter.
  6. Add the carrots and garlic and cook in the butter for 5 minutes.
  7. Add the mince to the pot and brown the mince.
  8. Add the seasonings, peas and broth to the pot and stir.
  9. When the mince has browned, remove from the heat and set aside.
  10. When the potatoes are ready, use a cauldron to drain the water from the potatoes. Add the potatoes back into the pot. Add a tablespoon of butter, season with salt and pepper, and add the coconut milk. Mash the sweet potato until the potatoes have a creamy texture.
  11. Add the mince filling to a oven dish. Spread the mince filling evenly across the bottom of the dish.
  12. Add the sweet potato mash on top of the mince filling and spread evenly across all the filling.
  13. When the topping has been spread evenly, take a fork and gently rake the top of the sweet potato from one side to the next. Repeat this process going side by side until the whole surface of the potato has been raked.
  14. Add the pie to the oven and roast for 25 minutes
  15. After 25 minutes, turn off the oven and turn on the broiler. Move the shepherd's pie as close to the broiler as you can for 5 to 10 minutes until the top of the shepherd's pie has a nice crispy crust on top.
  16. Remove the pie and let sit for 5 minutes. Cut the pie down the longest middle and twice across the short side to make 6 even pieces. Serve and enjoy.
