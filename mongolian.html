MONGOLIAN BEEF BROCCOLI

Mongolian beef is delicious, but different recipes tend to use alot of sugar, this is where its sweet profile comes from after all. This recipe aims to capture that attribute through more natural means without losing what makes this dish so delicious in the first place. With fresh stir-fried broccoli adding a crunch that is sure to satisfy.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 pounds flank steak
  • olive oil
  • 1 onion
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 2 heads of broccoli
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon ginger
  • salt
  • 1/2 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup of honey
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • zest and juice from 1 orange

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 25mins

  1. Start by cutting flank steak against the grain into strips. Season with salt.
  2. Seperate the steak into two batches. Pour half of the cornstarch into a mixing bowl. Put one batch of the steak into the bowl with the cornstarch and coat the steak evenly.
  3. Put a frying pan on high heat and coat with olive oil.
  4. Place the steak in the pan when the oil is at the smoking point and flash fry for about 1-2 minutes, flip and fry for another minute. Set aside that batch and repeat the process for the other batch. Save the leftover cornstarch for later.
  5. When your steak is done, chop your onion and dice the garlic. Take a wok and place on medium heat. Add the onions and soften them, then add the garlic.
  6. Chop your broccoli, slice the tomatoes, and set aside while you ready the sauce.
  7. Add the soy sauce, honey, orange zest, juice, ginger, and water to a bowl and mix all the ingredients. When ready add the sauce to the wok with the onions and garlic.
  8. Bring the sauce to a gentle boil then add the broccoli and tomatoes.
  9. Stir everything together and let the broccoli mix with the sauce for about 3 minutes
  10. Add the steak you had set aside, and the remaining cornstarch. Mix everything and bring the sauce to a gentle boil, mixing frequently to thicken the sauce from the cornstarch. This should take about 5 to 10 mins.
  11. Serve with rice and enjoy.
