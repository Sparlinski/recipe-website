THAI RED CURRY

Thai curries are some of my favorites, bringing with them extra dimensions that differ from their Indian counterparts, and this dish is no different. Flil of flavor and heat that is sure to please the senses, made from simple and healthy ingredients.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 2 pounds flank steak
  • 1 onion
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 pound green beans
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 14.5 ounce can coconut oil
  • 3 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 2 teaspoons fish sauce

DIRECTIONSprep - 15mins | cook - 35mins

  1. Slice the onion, place a pot on medium heat, and add the coconut oil. When the oil is heated add the onions and soften the onions.
  2. While the onions are softening, cut the flank steak into cubes. Cut the flank steak against the grain to ensure the meat stays tender.
  3. Add the meat to the pot and brown.
  4. Add the curry paste, seasonings, and coconut milk to the pot. Stir and bring the pot to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. While the curry is simmering, chop the green beans. After the 20 minutes is up, add the green beans to the pot. Cover again and let it simmer for a further 15 minutes. Serve the curry with rice and enjoy.
