JUMBALAYA

This New Orleans-inspired dish is packed full of flavor. The key to this dish is being familiar with the chorizo you use. Chorizo can be salty so it is wise to be cautious in the amount of salt you add. How well your chorizo holds together will also contribute to this dish's profile. I like a chorizo that holds together and adds extra substance providing a notable contrast of flavor in a bite to the chicken and vegetables in the dish.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 pounds chicken breast
  • 1 pound of chorizo
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 onion
  • 2 zucchini
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 1/2 to 1 tablespoon salt (depending on the saltiness of chorizo)
  • 1/2 tablespoon pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cajun seasoning
  • 2 large bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 14.5 ounce can of tomato sauce
  • 14.5 ounce can of diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup white wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon concentrated chicken broth

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 45mins

  1. Add a large pot with olive oil to medium heat.
  2. Dice onion and add to the pot when heated. Let the onions soften.
  3. Chop bell peppers and zucchini, dice the garlic, and add to the pot. Sautee at medium heat for 5-10 mins
  4. Dice chicken breast then add the diced chicken to pot.
  5. Slice the chorizo and add to the pot, if the chorizo has a casing ensure it is removed before adding to the pot.
  6. Add seasonings, tomato paste, tomato cans, vinegar, chicken broth, and water to the pot. Stir everything and bring the sauce to a boil.
  7. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover pot, and let simmer for 20 minutes.
  8. After 20 minutes stir the pot and recover, let simmer for additional 15 mins.
  9. After 15 more minutes increase heat to medium, remove lid, and let simmer for 10 more minutes stirring occasionally to reduce any excess liquid in the sauce.
  10. Serve with rice, pasta, or egg noodles.
