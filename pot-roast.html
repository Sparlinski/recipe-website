POT ROAST

This classic American dish is combined with influence from a traditional Irish stew. This recipe is intended for a slow cooker dump-and-go approach, but it can also be done stew style in one hour if you don't have a chance during your day to start the dishâ€”a fantastic, full-flavored filling meal with minimal fuss or effort.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 pounds chuck roast
  • 4-6 carrots
  • 2 parsnips
  • 1 onion
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 6-8 potatoes
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon oregano
  • 1 tablespoon basil
  • 1 tablespoon thyme
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 2 large bay leaves
  • 3 tablespoons worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon concentrated beef broth
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar

DIRECTIONSprep - 15mins | cook - 6hrs

  1. Cut chuck roast into cubes, trimming large parts of fat that may be present, and add to crock.
  2. Chop carrots, parsnips, garlic, and onions, and add to crock
  3. Cut potatoes into quarters and add to crock
  4. Add seasonings, vinegar, broth, and sauce to the crock, stir, and ensure that everything is at least 3/4 quarters way submerged, add a little extra water if not.
  5. Set heat to low and the timer to 6 hours.
