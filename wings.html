CRISPY BAKED WINGS WITH WEDGES

These wings are crispy, juicy, and full of flavor. Perfectly paired with potato wedges and roasted brussel sprouts in balsamic vinegar with a side of your favorite dipping sauce.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • FOR THE WINGS
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 4 pounds chicken wings
  • 1 tablspoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon pepper flakes
  • FOR THE WEDGES
  • 2 medium potatoes (per person)
  • salt
  • pepper
  • paprika
  • garlic powder
  • olive oil
  • FOR BRUSSEL SPROUTS
  • salt
  • pepper
  • balsamic vinegar

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 35mins

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees and begin to prep potatoes.
  2. Slice the potatoes into even-sized wedges. Place cut potatoes into a large bowl with cold water with some ice cubes. Leave in the water while the chicken and brussels sprouts are prepped.
  3. For the chicken wings, trim the wings by cutting off wing tips, and separating the wingette and drumette. Pat the wings with a paper towel making sure to remove all excess juice. Toss the trimmed wings into a large bowl.
  4. Add all the seasonings for the wings into the bowl. Mix the wings and seasonings with your hand until they are evenly coated. Set aside the wings to prep the sprouts and finish the potatoes.
  5. Wash the sprouts, trim the ends, and cut them in half. Place the sprouts into a baking dish. Season them with salt and pepper, and drizzle a generous amount of balsamic vinegar and olive oil. Toss the sprouts until they are evenly coated with the seasonings and dressings. Set aside and finish prepping the potatoes.
  6. Drain the water from the potatoes and pat dry them with a paper towel.
  7. Place the wedges back into the bowl after you have dried it. Season the wedges with the spices and toss them in the bowl until they are evenly coated. Drizzle with olive oil and toss again until they are evenly coated with oil.
  8. Place the wedges on a baking dish. Now you can add everything to the oven. Place the wings on a greased cooling rack, and place a baking sheet under them to catch any drippings. Place the wedges and sprouts in the oven and set the timer for 20 minutes.
  9. After 20 minutes, remove the wedges and turn them over to the other side. Return the wedges to the oven and set the timer for another 15 minutes.
  10. When the 15 minutes is up, remove the wings from the oven and set aside to cool.
  11. Let the sprouts and wedges finish for another 5 minutes while the wings cool then serve and enjoy.
