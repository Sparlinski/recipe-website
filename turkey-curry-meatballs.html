THAI TURKEY CURRY MEATBALLS

This recipe is heavily inspired by Thai cuisine with a compliment of roasted sweet potatoes to pair with the delicious lemongrass gravy. Just the right amount of heat brings this dish together, with the sweetness of the potato pairing with the curry-infused turkey meatballs.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • FOR THE MEATBALLS
  • 2 lbs ground turkey 85% lean
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1/2 cup alond meal
  • FOR THE GRAVY
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 2 shallots
  • 4 tablespoons lemongrass paste
  • 14.5 ounce can coconut oil
  • 2 tablespoons arrowroot starch
  • 1/2 tablespoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon concentrated chicken broth
  • 1 cup of water
  • FOR THE POTATOES
  • 7 ounces sweet potato (per person)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • salt and pepper
  • cinnamon

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 35mins

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Peel and chop sweet potatoes into small dice-sized cubes.
  3. Place potatoes on an oven pan, coat in olive oil, sprinkle salt, pepper, and cinnamon until lightly dusted. Place the pan in the oven.
  4. While potatoes are in the oven, start on the meatballs. Combine all the ingredients in a large bowl and mix well with your hands.
  5. Shape meatball mixture into golf ball-sized meatballs. If you want to be accurate in the size, use a 1/4 measuring cup to measure out the mixture. Place each of the meatballs into an oven dish.
  6. Reduce the heat in the oven to 375 degrees and place the meatballs in the oven. Set a timer for 10 minutes.
  7. After 10 minutes, remove the sweet potatoes from the oven and turn them over to their opposite side. Return them to the oven and set the timer for 25 minutes.
  8. While the sweet potatoes and meatballs are in the oven, work on the lemongrass gravy. Start by dicing the shallots.
  9. Put a medium-sized pot on medium heat and add the coconut oil. Add the shallots and fry them for 5 minutes.
  10. After 5 mins add the lemon grass paste and continue to fry the shallots, stirring regularly until they soften.
  11. Add the coconut milk, water, broth, salt, and red pepper flakes. Increase the heat and bring to a boil.
  12. When the sauce starts to boil, reduce the heat to medium-low. Mix the arrowroot starch in a little water first before adding to the sauce. Stir the gravy until it thickens. Reduce the heat to low and keep on heat until the meatballs and potatoes are ready, stirring occasionally.
  13. Serve meatballs with a ladle of gravy and a side of your choice of vegetable.
