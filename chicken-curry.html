CHICKEN CURRY

This recipe has evolved through trial and error to deliver a delicious curry, removing the added sugar that some can bring without sacrificing any flavor. As with all curries, the type of powder you use is king, so don't be afraid to experiment to augment the flavor.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 pounds chicken breast
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 2 potatoes
  • 14.5 ounce chick peas
  • 1/2 cup of raisins
  • 1 cup of medium sugarfree salsa
  • 2 tablespoons ginger powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili flakes
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 4 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 14.5 ounce can of coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon concentrated chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons arrowroot flour
  • 1 dollop of natural yogurt (for serving)

DIRECTIONSprep - 25mins | cook - 55mins

  1. Add large pot to medium heat and add coconut oil.
  2. Chop onion and add to the pot, fry for 5 mins.
  3. Dice and trim chicken breast then add to pot.
  4. Add curry powder and coat chicken and onions in powder, increase heat to medium-high, and fry onions and chicken for 5 mins.
  5. Chop bell peppers and add to the pot.
  6. Add coconut milk, remaining seasonings, spices, broth, salsa and raisins. Stir and increase heat to high. Bring sauce to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and let simmer for 15 minutes.
  7. While curry is simmering, peel and dice potatoes into acorn-sized pieces.
  8. After 15mins, remove lid and stir. Return lid and simmer for 5 mins.
  9. Add potatoes to curry and simmer for 15mins
  10. After 15 mins remove the lid, mix arrowroot flour in a cup with a small amount of water until mixed. Add arrowroot to the curry and stir until mixed.
  11. Leave the lid off the pot and increase heat to medium. Let simmer for 10 more minutes ensuring to stir occasionally.
  12. Serve with rice and a dollap of natural yogurt.
