BEEF CURRY

Much like its chicken counterpart, this recipe evolved through trial and error to deliver a delicious curry, removing the added sugar that some can bring without sacrificing any flavor. The difference is slight, but the taste profile of the beef, and added mushrooms make a differentiation to its flavor.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • 2 pounds stew beef
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 tray of mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup of raisins
  • 1 cup of medium sugarfree salsa
  • 2 tablespoons ginger powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili flakes
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 4 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 14.5 ounce can of coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon concentrated beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons arrowroot flour
  • 1 dollop of natural yogurt (for serving)

DIRECTIONSprep - 20mins | cook - 35mins

  1. Add large pot to medium heat and add coconut oil.
  2. Chop onion and add to the pot, fry for 5 mins.
  3. Cut stew beef into smaller pieces if they are too big.
  4. Add curry powder and coat beef and onions in powder, increase heat to medium-high, and fry onions and beef for 5 mins.
  5. Chop bell peppers and add to the pot.
  6. Wash mushrooms, remove stem and slice.
  7. Add coconut milk, remaining seasonings, spices, broth, salsa and raisins. Add mushrooms the stir and increase heat to high. Bring sauce to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and let simmer for 15 minutes.
  8. While curry is simmering, peel and dice potatoes into acorn-sized pieces.
  9. After 15mins, remove lid and stir. Return lid and simmer for 5 mins.
  10. Add potatoes to curry and simmer for 15mins
  11. After 15 mins remove the lid, mix arrowroot flour in a cup with a small amount of water until mixed. Add arrowroot to the curry and stir until mixed.
  12. Leave the lid off the pot and increase heat to medium. Let simmer for 10 more minutes ensuring to stir occasionally.
  13. Serve with rice and a dollap of natural yogurt.
