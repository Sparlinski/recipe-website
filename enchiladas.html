ENCHILADAS

These enchiladas bring all the flavor and leave the extras at the door. Making the spices and sauces from scratch ensures a cleaner final dish with none of the flavor sacrificed. Pair with roasted brussel sprouts and spanish rice to bring a rounded healthy take on this Mexican classic.

INGREDIENTSserves 5-6

  • FOR THE TACO SEASONING
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon of oregano
  • FOR THE ENCHILADA SAUCE
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablspoon oregano
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 3 cups chicken
  • 3 tablespoons arrow root starch
  • 6 ounce can tomato paste
  • teaspoon salt
  • teaspoon pepper
  • FOR ENCHILADAS
  • 2 pounds chicken breast
  • 1 onion
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 6 large wholewheat flour tortillas
  • 1 cup of cheddar cheese

DIRECTIONSprep - 30mins | cook - 35mins

  1. Preheat oven to 380 degrees.
  2. Add all taco seasoning ingredients into a large bowl and mix together.
  3. Dice the chicken breast then add the chicken breast to the large bowl with the mixed taco seasoning.
  4. With your hands, mix the diced chicken thourally with the taco seasoning until the chicken is evenly coated.
  5. Add olive oil to a large pan on medium-high heat.
  6. Add the seasoned chicken to the pan and fry for 3 mins on either side of the chicken.
  7. Return the chicken to the bowl and set aside.
  8. Dice and chop the onions and the peppers. In the same pan you fried the chicken, add the onion and peppers. Saute the onions and peppers for 5 minutes until softened and seasoned with the remnants of the spices in the pan. Turn the heat off on the pan when ready and set aside also.
  9. While the vegetables are sauteeing, mix the spices for the enchilada sauce in a bowl. Add a pan on medium-high heat with some olive oil and then add the mixed enchilada spices. Stir the spices frequently until the spices are toasted and fragrant.
  10. Add the chicken broth, arrowroot starch, and tomato paste to the pan, stir the sauce, and mix all the ingredients. Let the sauce come to a gentle boil, stirring frequently.
  11. When the sauce is ready, add the chicken set aside to the vegetables in the pan, then add 3/4's of the sauce to the pan. Add the cheese, and turn the heat to medium on the pan. Stir all the ingredients together until the cheese melts into the sauce.
  12. Take a baking dish, take a tortilla wrap, and place it in the baking dish, scoop about a cup size serving of the enchilada filling onto the wrap. Fold the wrap on one side over the other. Take a spoonful of the remainder enchilada sauce and pour it over where the two sides of the folded wrap meet. Repeat this process, placing each wrap in the dish side by side. When all the wraps are filled and folded, sprinkle some extra cheese over the top of them all.
  13. Set the enchiladas into the oven and bake for 20 minutes.
  14. Serve with roasted brussel sprouts and Mexican rice and enjoy.
